Reason why people invest in the Top 100 Crypto coins

Nov 7th 2023 4 Min read

The crypto market is one of the most dynamic and exciting sectors in the world of finance. With thousands of different coins and tokens available, each with its own features and functions, the crypto industry offers a wide range of opportunities and challenges for investors. However, among the vast array of crypto assets, there is a group of coins that stand out from the rest: the top 100 crypto coins.

The top 100 crypto coins are the ones that have the highest market capitalization, which is the total value of all the coins in circulation. The market capitalization of a coin is calculated by multiplying the price of the coin by the number of coins in circulation. The top 100 crypto coins are constantly changing, as new coins enter or exit the list, or as the prices and supplies of the existing coins fluctuate. However, some of the coins that have consistently remained in the top 100 are Bitcoin, ether, Binance coin, Cardano, polkadot, tether, and Solana.

But why do people invest in the top 100 crypto coins? What are the benefits and risks of investing in these coins? What are the factors that influence the performance and popularity of these coins? In this article, we will explore some of the most common reasons why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins, and how they differ from investing in other crypto assets.

Speculation

One of the main reasons why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins is to speculate on the price and hope for high returns in a short time. Many crypto investors are attracted by the volatility and potential growth of the crypto market, especially the coins that have a large market capitalization and liquidity. Liquidity refers to the ease of buying and selling a coin in the market, without affecting its price too much. The more liquid a coin is, the easier it is to trade it, and the lower the transaction costs and risks are.

The top 100 crypto coins tend to have higher liquidity than other coins, as they have more buyers and sellers, more trading platforms, and more market exposure. This means that investors can buy and sell these coins more quickly and efficiently, and take advantage of the price movements and trends in the market. Some of the top 100 coins, such as Bitcoin, ether, and Binance coin, have experienced significant price increases in the past few years, making them appealing to speculators.

However, speculation also involves a high level of risk, as the prices of the top 100 crypto coins can also drop sharply and unpredictably, due to various factors, such as market sentiment, regulation, competition, hacking, or technical issues. Therefore, investors who speculate on the top 100 crypto coins need to have a good understanding of the market dynamics, the fundamentals and technicals of the coins, and the risk management strategies, such as diversification, stop-loss, and take-profit orders.

Diversification and Hedging

Another reason why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins is to diversify their portfolios and hedge against inflation. Diversification is the practice of spreading oneâ€™s investments across different assets, sectors, or markets, to reduce the overall risk and increase the expected return. Hedging is the practice of using one asset to offset the potential losses or risks of another asset, such as using a derivative contract or a short position.

Some crypto investors see crypto as a way to diversify their assets and reduce their exposure to traditional financial markets, such as stocks, bonds, and fiat currencies. They may believe that crypto can offer a higher return than other investments, or that it can protect their wealth from inflation, devaluation, or government interference. Inflation is the persistent increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy over a period of time. Devaluation is the deliberate reduction in the value of a currency relative to other currencies, usually by the central bank or the government. Government interference is the intervention or manipulation of the financial system by the authorities, such as imposing taxes, regulations, sanctions, or bans.

Some of the top 100 crypto coins, such as bitcoin, ether, and tether, have been regarded as digital gold, digital oil, or digital cash, respectively, by some investors. Bitcoin is often seen as digital gold, as it has a limited supply of 21 million coins, and it is not controlled by any central authority. Ether is often seen as a digital oil, as it fuels the Ethereum network, which is a platform for decentralized applications and smart contracts. Tether is often seen as a digital cash, as it is a stablecoin that is pegged to the US dollar, and it is used as a medium of exchange and a store of value in the crypto market.

However, diversification and hedging also have their limitations and challenges, as the top 100 crypto coins are not immune to the external factors that affect the crypto industry, such as regulation, competition, hacking, or technical issues. Moreover, the correlation between the top 100 crypto coins and other assets, such as stocks, bonds, or fiat currencies, is not fixed or stable, and it can change over time, depending on the market conditions and events. Therefore, investors who diversify and hedge with the top 100 crypto coins need to monitor the market closely and adjust their portfolios accordingly.

Support and Innovation

A third reason why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins is to support the vision and innovation of the crypto industry. Support is the act of showing interest, approval, or encouragement for a cause, project, or person. Innovation is the act of creating, developing, or introducing something new, original, or improved, that adds value or solves a problem.

Some crypto investors are motivated by the ideology and technology behind the crypto projects, rather than the financial gains. They may believe that crypto can create a more decentralized, transparent, and fair financial system, or that it can enable new possibilities and solutions for various sectors and problems. They may also admire the teams, communities, or leaders behind the crypto projects, and want to contribute to their success and development.

Some of the top 100 crypto coins, such as Cardano, polka dot, and Solana, have been developed with the aim of improving the scalability, interoperability, and functionality of the crypto ecosystem. Scalability is the ability of a system to handle a large number of transactions or users without compromising its performance or security. Interoperability is the ability of a system to communicate and exchange information or value with other systems, without losing its integrity or functionality. Functionality is the ability of a system to provide a range of features and services that meet the needs and expectations of the users or customers.

However, support and innovation also entail a high level of uncertainty and complexity, as the top 100 crypto coins are often based on novel and experimental technologies, that may not be fully tested, proven, or adopted by the market. Moreover, the top 100 crypto coins face a lot of competition and challenges from other crypto projects, as well as from the traditional financial system, that may hinder or threaten their growth and innovation. Therefore, investors who support and innovate with the top 100 crypto coins need to have a long-term vision and a strong conviction, as well as a willingness to learn and adapt to the changing environment.

Conclusion

In conclusion, there are many reasons why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins, but some of the most common ones are speculation, diversification hedging, and support and innovation. Each of these reasons has its own benefits and risks, and each of them requires a different approach and mindset from the investors. However, these are not the only reasons why people invest in the top 100 crypto coins, and different investors may have different goals, preferences, and strategies. Therefore, before investing in the top 100 crypto coins, it is important to do your own research, analysis, and evaluation, and to understand your own risk appetite, objectives, and expectations.
