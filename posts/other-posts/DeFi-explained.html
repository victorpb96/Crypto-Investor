DeFi explained today

Nov 7th 2023 4 Min read

DeFi is a shortened version of the words decentralised finance. It is geared towards trust mechanisms, to replace traditional intermediaries using blockchains, and internet-native financial systems.

With DeFi it enables us to essentially cut out the middlemen such as large financial institutions such as banks and the stock exchange. This is replaced by software that carries out the transactions that occur between people as they trade directly with one another, which can also be called P2P. It uses blockchain-based (smart contracts) that manage to ensure that the process is trustworthy and fair by settling trades and making markets.

With DeFi you’re able to do various things that banks are able to support - buy insurance, trade assets, trade derivatives, lend, borrow, earn interest and even more. In actual fact, it is faster and doesn’t require a third party or paperwork.

The goal with DeFi is to be a part of the cryptocurrency ecosystem and change the way we see and use our crypto, it has been designed and developed to be far more efficient than the outdated systems the banks use and the code is more up to scratch.

There are various benefits that come with utilising the incorporated DeFi system such as fast interest rates and rewards that will often rapidly update “every 15 seconds” which is far improved from that of the traditional Wall Street.

The transactions are also transparent, everyone involved can see the full set of transactions whereas private institutions don’t usually grant this sort of transparency.

You will find there is a level of flexibility involved with being able to move your assets anywhere at any time, without the need to ask for permission, paying quite expensive fees, or waiting for long transfers to complete.

You don’t need to provide personal information - address, email, or your name.

There’s no hassle of having to open an account you simply create a wallet on a crypto exchange such as LBank.

People are getting involved in different ways with DeFi today - buying derivatives (which are set between two or more parties that can trade on an exchange or over-the-counter (OTC)), saving for their future, getting loans, trading and lending.
